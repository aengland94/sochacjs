About James

The best resource available to make the Earth a better place to live is a wise mind seeking the truth with a sincere heart.

He received his B.S. degree from the University of Michigan – Dearborn in Mathematics. Not wanting to work for a corporation, he entered graduate school at the University of Wyoming in Mathematics. There he received a M.S. degree in Mathematics and then a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics.

His first ‘job’ was a post-doctoral visiting professor at the University of California – Davis. He did that for 3 years and then applied for a tenure-track position. He is now a full professor in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at that university; James Madison University. Also, he is currently the director of the Center for Computational Mathematics and Modeling at JMU.

He has worked in several areas of applied mathematics. His main interest is in helping others to learn applied mathematics to help them solve their problems and to help make the Earth a better place to live. He especially enjoys studying initial value ordinary and partial differential equations. He has publications in several areas of applied mathematics and has received several grants to study areas of applied mathematics.
